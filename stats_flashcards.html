Probability Rules

For events A and B: P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A \cap B) P(A^c) = 1 − P(A) P(A | B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} when P(B) > 0.

Discrete Distributions

The Binomial distribution models the number of successes in n independent Bernoulli trials: P(X = k) = {n \choose k} p^k (1−p)^{n−k}. The Poisson distribution models the number of events in a fixed interval: P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^k}{k!}.

Normal Distribution & Z‑Scores

A continuous distribution characterized by mean μ and standard deviation σ. Standardize values using z = \frac{x − μ}{σ} to compare across different scales. Approximately 68% of values lie within ±1σ, 95% within ±2σ, and 99.7% within ±3σ of the mean.

Confidence Intervals

A 95% confidence interval for the population mean μ is given by: \bar{x} ± z_{\alpha/2} \frac{σ}{\sqrt{n}} when population variance is known, or \bar{x} ± t_{\alpha/2, n−1} \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} when using sample variance.

Hypothesis Testing

To test a claim about a parameter, set up null and alternative hypotheses, choose a significance level, compute a test statistic (z or t), and compare to critical values or use a p‑value approach. Reject the null hypothesis if the test statistic falls in the rejection region.

Regression & Correlation

Simple linear regression fits a line y = β_0 + β_1 x to data by minimizing squared errors. The coefficient of determination R^2 measures the proportion of variance explained. The correlation coefficient r quantifies the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables.
